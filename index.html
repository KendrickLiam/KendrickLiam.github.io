Encryption & HTTPS

Encryption is the process of converting information into a secret code so that only authorized parties can read it. On the internet, HTTPS (HyperText Transfer Protocol Secure) uses encryption to protect data exchanged between your browser and a website. This prevents attackers from eavesdropping, tampering with, or stealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers. The small lock icon you see in your browser’s address bar means the site is using HTTPS and your connection is secure.
